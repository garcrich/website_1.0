THE JOURNEY

Sharing is my Passion

Being able to share your thoughts and ideas has always been something I’ve been obsessive about.

From blog posts to YouTube videos, I’ve always been fascinated by giving and receiving information.

There is no better place for this than the World Wide Web. My fire to share drives me every day to learn new ways to get information online and enrich other’s lives.

How it Began

After I graduated college I has to face the cold hard truth, I didn’t have skills that produced tangible results.

Having this realization shook me to the core. I believed in the American dream, work hard, go college and you will have a great life. This wasn’t the case.

I was fortunate enough to get a job at a credit union providing online support for customers.

At my new job I was faced with many mundane challenges that I had to perform on a daily basis.

I found myself constantly clicking, waiting, and typing the same thing over and over. For my own sanity I had to find a better way to complete my work.

Learning to Learn

It was at this point that I decided to learn Microsoft Office deeply.

I used the Microsoft suite every day and the majority of my tasks were completed with these programs.

I found search the web for resources, read countless books, found forums, and watched online tutorials.

Eventually I because a certified Microsoft Office Specialist and became the expert within my company. I helped solved problems ranging from setting up templates for emails, reformatting Frequently Asked Question form, fixing PowerPoint presentations, and much more.

This knew found knowledge was powerful, and I was able to learn this on my own.

Mastering Learning

I had always wanted to try my hand in graphic design so I learned the basics, form, typography, drawing, color, graphic design software.

Over the course a year I found myself enjoying creating a few things here and there but never digging too deep.

I got rejected, again, and again… and again.

Being tired of my entry level job which I was over qualified for I applied for positions as a Marketer and a Technical Writer.

Every time I was asked the question, “Do you know HTML?” Well, I guess. About the only thing I knew about HTML was that it was the backbone of the web.

After being underqualified I decided to learn HTML, with HTML came another language CSS, and with this came JavaScript, and the technologies kept coming.

From writing a basic page that simple said, “Hello world!” to the web page you see today I made a conscious effort to always improve.

The most important lesson I’ve ever learned is, never stopped learning, and I never will.

What I’m doing now

Currently I’m improving my skills with graphic design, bootstrap, and JavaScript.

A hybrid of both foundational and advanced skills will only strengthen my skill set while keeping my current.

In addition to this I’m working for myself on a project by project basis.
